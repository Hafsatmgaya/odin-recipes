Party Jollof Recipe

Description

Party jollof rice with chicken is a flavorful and vibrant West African dish, known for its rich taste, smoky aroma, and perfectly seasoned rice. The rice is cooked in a well-spiced tomato sauce made with blended peppers, tomatoes, onions, and spices, giving it that signature party jollof flavor. The chicken is usually seasoned, fried, or grilled, adding a delicious and savory complement to the rice.

This dish is often served at celebrations and gatherings, making it a true crowd favorite. The combination of soft, well-cooked rice and tender, juicy chicken makes it satisfying and enjoyable for everyone. Whether cooked over firewood for that authentic smoky taste or on a stove, party jollof with chicken remains one of the most loved meals in West Africa.

  • Rice
  • Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • Tomatoes
  • Red bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • Onions
  • Tomato paste
  • Vegetable oil
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Chicken stock
  • Thyme
  • Curry powder
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes (stock cubes)

Steps

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and set aside.
  2. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and onions into a smooth mixture.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a pot and fry chopped onions.
  4. Add tomato paste and fry for a few minutes.
  5. Pour in the blended mixture and cook until it thickens and reduces.
  6. Add seasoning cubes, salt, thyme, curry, and bay leaves.
  7. Pour in chicken stock and bring to a boil.
  8. Add the washed rice and stir well.
  9. Cover and cook on low heat until the rice is soft and fully cooked.
  10. Serve hot with fried or grilled chicken.
